# Koshiv Roadlines Website Structure

## 1. Website Goal
The website exists to present Koshiv Roadlines as a professional road transport brand, generate customer interest, and capture inquiries for bus service, truck service, and contract transport.

## 2. Website Type
V1 is a polished one-page frontend website built with React + TypeScript + Vite + Tailwind CSS. It is designed for GitHub Pages hosting and focuses on clear service messaging rather than full application functionality.

## 3. Approved Homepage Sections
The homepage will be a single-page experience with clearly separated sections and a fixed or sticky header/navbar for navigation.

## 4. Header / Navbar
- Brand logo and company name
- Navigation links to page sections
- Optional action button for inquiry/contact
- Responsive mobile support

## 5. Hero Section
- Strong headline for Koshiv Roadlines
- Short value proposition
- Supporting subtext describing bus and truck transport capabilities
- Primary call to action for inquiries

## 6. Services Overview
- A summary of the main service categories
- Quick visual or icon-based highlights for Bus Service, Truck Service, and Contract Transport
- Short statements of reliability, coverage, and safety

## 7. Bus Service Section
- Dedicated overview of bus transport services
- Core features such as passenger travel, charter routes, comfort, and safety
- Emphasis on routes, schedules, and trustworthiness

## 8. Truck Service Section
- Dedicated overview of truck transport services
- Core features such as cargo movement, logistics support, and freight handling
- Emphasis on reliability, timeliness, and scalable transport options

## 9. Contract Transport Section
- Dedicated overview of contract transport offerings
- Features such as long-term transport partnerships, dedicated fleets, and tailored logistics
- Benefits for businesses needing consistent road transport solutions

## 10. Routes & Coverage Section
- High-level description of regions served
- Coverage map or list of supported cities/routes for both bus and truck services
- Emphasis on geographic reliability and transport reach

## 11. Why Choose Koshiv Roadlines
- Customer-centric reasons to select the brand
- Points like safety, punctuality, service quality, and experienced operations
- Trust signals such as founder credibility, service commitment, and local expertise

## 12. About Section
- Short introduction to the company and founder/CEO Koushal
- Mission statement and service philosophy
- Business positioning as a road transport leader for buses and trucks

## 13. Inquiry / Contact Section
- Inquiry form placeholder or contact call-to-action
- Contact details such as email, phone, and domain reference
- Simple mechanism for visitors to request service information

## 14. Footer
- Copyright and company name
- Minimal legal or copyright line
- Secondary navigation or quick contact reference

## 15. What the Website Must Do
- Communicate Koshiv Roadlines brand and services clearly
- Support a responsive one-page layout
- Provide a strong call to action for inquiries
- Present bus, truck, and contract transport offerings distinctly
- Serve as an initial marketing and contact website for V1

## 16. What the Website Must Not Do in V1
- Do not implement full booking or scheduling systems
- Do not add user authentication or account flows
- Do not add complex routing beyond section anchors
- Do not introduce a backend API in V1
- Do not serve as a complete enterprise portal yet

## 17. Future Expansion Pages
- Dedicated Bus Service page with route details and schedules
- Dedicated Truck Service page with cargo, pricing, and fleet overview
- Contract Transport page with business onboarding and case studies
- About / Team page with founder story and company history
- Contact page with form submission and support details
- Admin or operations portal once backend is introduced

## Notes for Future Developers
- Keep the initial frontend strictly within `frontend/`
- Preserve `frontend/docs/README.md` and document-level assets
- Design components as reusable sections for future expansion
- Keep V1 focused on marketing, clarity, and responsiveness
- Plan backend integration later with Java Spring Boot, PostgreSQL, and Docker
